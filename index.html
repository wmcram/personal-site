Here's some "takes" on various topics:

  • While I believe AI will transform the landscape of the software industry and many adjacent sectors, I don't think we will acheive ASI in my lifetime. This isn't even because I think it's technically impossible, rather that superintelligence can't (and shouldn't) spontaneously arise through largely "verbal" communication.
  • The fact that BB(6) and higher are "physically unrealizable" in conjunction with the fact that many nontrivial mathematical statements require >5 states to "encode" into a Turing Machine indicates that abstraction and changes in perspective are a fundamentally necessary part of truth-seeking.
  • By and large, college courses (not the rest of college) are a waste of time. A majority of the material can be learned for free on one's own time in a more efficient and comprehensive way. Furthermore, now that LLMs serve as a personal tutor, the traditional barriers to self-teaching are lower than ever. In an ideal world, students would learn in dialogue with an LLM, solve many problems socratically, and then take a test to demonstrate their knowledge.
  • Having a morning alarm is the single worst you can do for your overall health.
  • Many so-called "rationalists" would do well to go with their gut more often.
